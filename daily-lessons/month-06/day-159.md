# Day 159: Portfolio Project Planning - Choosing Your Project

## Section 1: Topic
**How to Pick a Database Project That Impresses Employers**

## Section 2: English Explanation
A strong portfolio project shows you can identify a real problem, design proper schema, write clean SQL, and explain decisions. Choose something with 5-8 tables, clear relationships, and practical queries that mirror actual business scenarios.

## Section 5: Requirements Gathering Exercise (30 min)
**Step 1:** Choose ONE project from above (or your own idea).

**Step 2:** Answer these planning questions:
1. Who are the main users of this system?
2. What data must be stored for each user type?
3. What actions happen most often (CRUD operations)?
4. What reports or dashboards are useful?
5. What are the business rules and constraints?
6. What data must NEVER be duplicated?

**Step 3:** List all entities (future tables):
```
Restaurant example: Customers, Menu Items, Categories,
Orders, Order Items, Tables, Staff, Payments
```

**Step 4:** Sketch rough relationships:
- Customer places many Orders (1:M)
- Order contains many Order Items (1:M)
- Menu Item appears in many Order Items (1:M)

## Section 7: Speaking Practice
"Before building the database, I need to understand your requirements. Who uses this system? What data do you track? What reports do you need? Then I can design tables and relationships matching your workflow."

## Section 8: Reading Practice
Browse 2-3 database portfolio projects on GitHub. Notice their README structure, diagrams, design explanations, and sample queries.

## Section 9: Review Task
- Finalize your project choice
- Write a 1-paragraph project description
- List all entities (minimum 5 tables)
- Identify at least 3 relationships
- Write 5 sample queries your project should support

## Section 10: Expected Result
You have chosen a project, defined its scope, identified major entities and relationships, and can explain why it demonstrates professional database skills. Ready for the design phase.
