# Day 24: WHERE with AND, OR Operators

## Topic
Combining multiple conditions in WHERE using AND and OR operators.

## English Explanation
AND means ALL conditions must be true. OR means at least ONE condition must be true. You can combine them to create complex filters. Use parentheses () to group conditions and control the order of evaluation. Without parentheses, AND is evaluated before OR, which can give unexpected results.

## Khmer Explanation
AND មានន័យថាលក្ខខណ្ឌទាំងអស់ត្រូវពិត។ OR មានន័យថាលក្ខខណ្ឌមួយត្រូវពិត។ ប្រើវង់ក្រចក () ដើម្បីដាក់ជាក្រុម និងគ្រប់គ្រងលំដាប់នៃការវាយតម្លៃ។

## SQL Example
```sql
-- AND: both conditions must be true
SELECT * FROM products
WHERE category = 'Electronics' AND price < 100;

-- OR: at least one condition must be true
SELECT * FROM products
WHERE category = 'Electronics' OR category = 'Books';

-- Combining AND and OR with parentheses
SELECT * FROM products
WHERE (category = 'Electronics' OR category = 'Books')
AND price < 50;

-- Without parentheses (different result!)
SELECT * FROM products
WHERE category = 'Electronics' OR category = 'Books' AND price < 50;
-- This means: Electronics(any price) OR (Books under 50)

-- Multiple AND conditions
SELECT * FROM employees
WHERE department = 'Sales' AND salary > 3000 AND status = 'active';
```

## Speaking Practice
Explain to a client:
"We can filter data using multiple conditions. AND means all conditions must match — like finding electronics under $50. OR means any condition can match — like finding customers in Phnom Penh or Siem Reap. We use parentheses to group conditions clearly."

## Reading Practice
Read this query and predict the result:
```sql
SELECT name, price, category FROM products
WHERE (category = 'Food' OR category = 'Drinks')
AND price > 5 AND price < 20;
```
Answer: Products that are Food or Drinks, with price between 5 and 20.
