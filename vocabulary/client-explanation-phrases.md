# Client Explanation Phrases

## How to Explain Database Concepts to Non-Technical Clients

### What is a database?
"A database is like a smart filing cabinet for your business data. Instead of searching through papers, the computer finds any information in less than a second."

### Why do you need a database?
"Right now your data might be in spreadsheets or paper files. A database organizes everything so you can search, filter, and update information instantly — even with millions of records."

### What is a table?
"Think of a table like one spreadsheet tab. Each table stores one type of thing — customers in one table, orders in another, products in another."

### What is a primary key?
"Every record gets a unique ID number — like a national ID card. This makes sure we never confuse one customer with another, even if they have the same name."

### What are constraints?
"Constraints are rules that protect your data. For example, we can make sure every customer has an email address, no two customers share the same email, and prices are never negative."

### What is SQL?
"SQL is the language we use to talk to the database. When you ask 'show me all orders from last month,' I write that as a SQL query and the database returns the answer instantly."

### Why separate tables instead of one big table?
"If we put everything in one table, data gets repeated and messy. Separate tables keep data organized — like having separate filing drawers for customers, orders, and products."

### What does UPDATE mean?
"UPDATE changes existing information. For example, if a customer changes their phone number, we update just that one field without touching anything else."

### What does NULL mean?
"NULL means we don't have that information yet. It's different from zero or blank — it means 'unknown.' For example, a customer who hasn't given us their phone number has NULL in that field."

### How do you protect the data?
"We use constraints to prevent bad data from entering the system. We also verify before making changes — like reading a record before deleting it to make sure we're removing the right one."
